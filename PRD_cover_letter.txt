Dear Editors of Physical Review D,

I am submitting the enclosed manuscript, "Glueball Mass Ratios from
Three Perpendicular Directions: A Geometric Derivation with Zero Free
Parameters," for consideration as a Regular Article.

The paper reports a new result: the six lightest glueball mass ratios
in pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory can be derived from the resonant mode
structure of three mutually perpendicular directions, using mode
indices drawn from {1, sqrt(2), 2} -- the edge, face diagonal, and
double edge of a unit cube. The derivation uses zero free parameters
and achieves a mean error of 1.0% against lattice QCD values.

The critical ratio m(2++)/m(0++) = sqrt(2) matches the lattice value
of 1.398 to within 1.2%. This ratio is the face diagonal of the cube
-- a purely geometric quantity requiring no knowledge of QCD.

The SU(3) gauge symmetry is derived (not assumed) from the
perpendicular geometry: U(3)/U(1) = SU(3). Independent empirical
support is provided from cosmic web data, where three-filament nodes
with mutually perpendicular geometry accumulate significantly more
mass (r = 0.397, p = 8.0 x 10^-37, N = 942).

The paper is directly relevant to Physical Review D as it addresses
the glueball spectrum -- a topic with an extensive publication history
in this journal, including the foundational lattice calculations of
Morningstar and Peardon (PRD 60, 034509) and Chen et al. (PRD 73,
014516) against which the predictions are compared.
